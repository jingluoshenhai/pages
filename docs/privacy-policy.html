Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 1, 2026 · Last updated: June 3, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Puzzle Grid (the "App", "we", "us") collects, uses, and shares information when you use our mobile game distributed on Google Play. By using the App you agree to the practices described below.

1. Who we are

Developer: YTY
Contact (privacy questions): privacy@yty-games.com
App package name: com.yty.puzzlegrid

2. Information we collect

Puzzle Grid is a single-player offline-friendly game. We do not ask you to create an account or provide your name, email, phone number, or location. However, third-party SDKs we integrate may automatically collect technical data to deliver ads and improve the app. The data categories are:

CategoryWhat is collectedPurpose
Advertising ID (GAID) Your device's resettable Google Advertising ID Ad personalization (with consent), frequency capping, fraud prevention
Device & technical info Device model, manufacturer, OS version, language, country, screen size, network type, app version, install source Compatibility, analytics, ad delivery
Coarse location Approximate country / region inferred from IP address (no precise GPS location) Ad targeting, regulatory compliance (GDPR/CCPA scope)
App interactions Levels started/completed, session length, in-app purchases (none currently), ad impressions and clicks, crash reports, button taps Performance monitoring, feature improvement, ad analytics
Crash & diagnostic data Stack traces, OS logs, app state at crash time Bug fixing, stability improvements
Local game data Level progress, coin balance, settings, item inventory — stored on your device only Save your gameplay locally; never transmitted to us

We do not collect: name, email address, phone number, contacts, photos, calendar, SMS, microphone audio, camera images, precise GPS location, biometric data, financial information, or files on your device.

3. Third-party services and SDKs

The App integrates the following Google services. Each service has its own privacy policy that governs how the data described above is processed:

3.1 Google AdMob (advertising)

AdMob serves banner, interstitial, rewarded, and app-open ads inside the App. AdMob may collect your Advertising ID, IP address, device info, ad interactions, and a non-precise location for serving relevant ads, frequency capping, and click-fraud prevention.

  • AdMob privacy policy: policies.google.com/technologies/ads
  • How Google uses information from sites/apps that use its services: policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites

3.2 Google User Messaging Platform (UMP) — GDPR/CCPA consent

For users in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, Switzerland, and certain U.S. states, we display a consent form on first launch using Google's UMP SDK. You can choose whether to receive personalized ads. Without consent, ads will be shown in a non-personalized form (NPA). You may revisit your choice anytime via the in-app Privacy Options entry in Settings.

3.3 Firebase Analytics & Firebase Crashlytics

We use Firebase Analytics to understand aggregate gameplay behaviour (e.g., which levels are most attempted) and Firebase Crashlytics to detect and diagnose app crashes. These collect device info, app interactions, and crash diagnostics. Data is associated with a randomly generated instance ID, not with your real identity.

  • Firebase privacy and security: firebase.google.com/support/privacy

4. How we use the information

  • To run the game and save your progress on your device.
  • To show advertising (personalized only with your consent).
  • To monitor crashes and fix bugs.
  • To analyse aggregate gameplay metrics so we can improve features and difficulty.
  • To detect fraudulent or abusive use of advertising.
  • To comply with applicable laws and respond to legal requests.

We do not sell or rent your personal information to third parties.

5. Children's privacy

Puzzle Grid is rated for general audiences but is not directed at children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at privacy@yty-games.com and we will take reasonable steps to delete it.

6. Your rights and choices

6.1 Reset / opt-out of advertising ID

You can reset or opt out of personalized ads at the OS level: Android Settings → Privacy / Google → Ads → tap "Delete advertising ID" or "Opt out of Ads Personalization".

6.2 Manage consent (EEA / UK / California)

Open the App, go to Settings, and tap Privacy Options to re-display the consent form and change your preferences at any time.

6.3 Other rights under GDPR / CCPA

Subject to applicable law, you have the right to access, correct, or delete personal data we hold about you, restrict or object to processing, and request data portability. To exercise these rights, email privacy@yty-games.com with the subject "Privacy Rights Request". We will respond within 30 days.

6.4 Delete local data

All on-device data (progress, coins, settings) can be cleared by uninstalling the App or by going to Android Settings → Apps → Puzzle Grid → Storage → Clear data.

7. Data retention

Local game data is stored on your device until you uninstall or clear it. Data processed by third-party services is retained according to their policies (typically 14 months for AdMob/Firebase aggregate data unless required for fraud prevention, legal, or accounting reasons).

8. International transfers

Data processed by Google services may be transferred to and processed in the United States or other countries where Google operates. Google relies on Standard Contractual Clauses and other safeguards approved by the European Commission for cross-border transfers.

9. Security

We rely on the security measures provided by the platforms (Google Play, Android, Firebase, AdMob) to protect data in transit (TLS) and at rest. No method of electronic storage is 100% secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy occasionally. Any change becomes effective when posted at the same URL (with an updated "Last updated" date). For material changes we will, where reasonably possible, surface a notice within the App.
